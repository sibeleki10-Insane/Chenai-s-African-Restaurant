Our Story

Where the Zambezi
Meets the Table

Nestled in the heart of Katima Mulilo — where five nations converge on the banks of the mighty Zambezi — Chenai's African Restaurant is a celebration of the rich culinary heritage of the Zambezi Region.

We bring you the true flavours of Namibia: time-honoured recipes, locally sourced ingredients, and the warm hospitality that the Caprivi Strip is famous for. Every dish tells the story of our land, our people, and our traditions.

What We Serve

A Taste of the Zambezi

Our menu is rooted in the traditional foods of the Zambezi Region — slow-cooked stews, mahangu, fresh river fish, and seasonal vegetables prepared with generations of knowledge.

Kapana
Smoky grilled beef strips, a beloved Namibian street food staple — served with fresh chakalaka relish and pap
Traditional
River Fish Stew
Freshwater fish from the Zambezi, slow-simmered with tomato, onion, and wild herbs in the traditional Caprivi style
Mains
Mahangu Porridge
Pearl millet porridge — the heartbeat of Namibian cuisine — smooth, nourishing, and deeply comforting
Traditional
Goat Stew
Tender free-range goat braised low and slow with aromatic spices, served over fluffy rice with leafy greens
Mains
Ifishimu (Dried Fish)
Sun-dried Zambezi fish, a cherished Lozi delicacy — rehydrated and cooked with onion and tomatoes in the age-old tradition
Traditional
Chicken in Groundnut Sauce
Free-range chicken slowly cooked in a rich, velvety groundnut sauce — a Southern African classic full of warmth
Mains
Morogo (Wild Spinach)
Indigenous leafy greens sautéed with garlic and peanut oil — a nutritious side connecting every meal to the land
Sides
Chakalaka Relish
A vibrant, spiced vegetable relish with beans, peppers, and tomato — adds colour and boldness to any plate
Sides
Pumpkin Leaves
Tender young pumpkin leaves cooked in groundnut paste — a beloved vegetable of the Zambezi
Sides
Maheu
